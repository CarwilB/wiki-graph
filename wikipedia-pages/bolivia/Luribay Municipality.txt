'''Luribay Municipality''' is the first [[Municipalities of Bolivia|municipal section]] of the [[Loayza Province]] in the [[La Paz Department (Bolivia)|La Paz Department]] of Bolivia. Encompassing an area of approximately {{cvt|506.3|km2}}, it had a population of 10,581 in March 2024. The town of [[Luribay]] serves as the administrative center of the municipality. Situated in the inter-Andean valleys, it is characterized by a dry climate and fertile soils, making it particularly suitable for certain agricultural activities and known for its [[wine]] and [[singani]] production.

== History ==
Luribay's history is deeply intertwined with the development of [[viticulture]] in Bolivia. The cultivation of grapes and the production of wine in Luribay date back to the colonial period, introduced by the [[Spanish Empire|Spanish]].<ref name="Wine">{{cite web|url=https://www.americaeconomia.com/negocios-e-industrias/el-vino-boliviano-busca-expandirse-en-el-mercado-internacional|title=El vino boliviano busca expandirse en el mercado internacional|work=AméricaEconomía|access-date=30 June 2025}}</ref>

== Geography ==
The municipality spans about {{cvt|506.3|km2}} within the [[Loayza Province]] in the [[La Paz Department, Bolivia|La Paz Department]].<ref name="Pop">{{cite web|url=https://www.citypopulation.de/en/bolivia/admin/la_paz/020901__luribay/|title=Luribay Municip ality boundaries|work=CityPopulation.de|access-date=17 June 2025}}</ref> The district consists of the main urban center of the town of [[Luribay]] and multiple villages.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://instad.bj/images/.../Cahier%20Village%20RGPH4%202013.pdf|title=Census list|work=INSAE census booklet|access-date=17 June 2025}}</ref> It is situated in the inter-Andean valleys (valles interandinos), a geographical zone between the higher altitudes of the Altiplano and the lower tropical plains. It has a landscape of valleys and slopes, contrasting with the high-altitude plains of the Altiplano and the flat expanses of the Amazonian lowlands.<ref name="Info">{{cite web|url=https://www.bolivia.travel/municipalidad/luribay|title=Luribay|work=Bolivia.travel|access-date=30 June 2025}}</ref> The town of Luribay is located at {{cvt|2546|m}}, while nearby mountain village of [[Anchallani]] is located at {{cvt|4187|m}}.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.db-city.com/Bolivia--La-Paz--Loayza--Luribay|title=Luribay|work=DB‑City.com|access-date=17 June 2025}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.db-city.com/Bolivia--La-Paz--Loayza--Luribay|title=Anchallani|work=DB‑City.com|access-date=17 June 2025}}</ref> The municipality is part of a region known for its dry climate and fertile soils.<ref name="Wine"/> The municipality incorporates several high mountains.<ref name=igm1>IGM map 1:50,000 6042-II Sicasica</ref><ref name=igm2>IGM map 1:50,000 6042-I Luribay</ref><ref name=ine>{{cite web|url=http://www.ine.gob.bo/publicaciones/visorPdf.aspx?Codigo=020901&tipo=1 |title=Luribay |publisher=INE, Bolivia |access-date=February 9, 2016 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160215030618/http://www.ine.gob.bo/publicaciones/visorPdf.aspx?Codigo=020901&tipo=1 |archive-date=15 February 2016 }}</ref>

== Demographics ==
Encompassing an area of approximately {{cvt|506.3|km2}}, the district had a population of 10,581 in March 2024. The population consisted of 5,685 males, and 5,454 females with a 92% literacy rate.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.citypopulation.de/en/bolivia/admin/la_paz/020901__luribay/|title=Luribay|work=CityPopulation.de|access-date=17 June 2025}}</ref> The economy is predominantly agricultural, with diverse crop cultivation. In addition to wine, Luribay is also known for producing [[singani]], a traditional Bolivian grape brandy.<ref name="Wine"/><ref name="Info"/>
