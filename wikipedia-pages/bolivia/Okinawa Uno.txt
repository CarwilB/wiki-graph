'''Okinawa Uno''', also called {{lang|es|'''Colonia Okinawa'''}} or simply '''Okinawa''', is a small city and [[Municipalities of Bolivia|municipality]] of [[Bolivia]], located in [[Ignacio Warnes Province]] in [[Santa Cruz Department (Bolivia)|Santa Cruz Department]]. The town is between the [[Río Grande (Bolivia)|Río Grande]] to the east and the Pailón River to the west. The municipality has a population of 12,482 inhabitants, according to the [[2012 Bolivian census]].<ref name=ine>{{cite web|access-date=12 Jan 2023|language=es|publisher=[[National Institute of Statistics of Bolivia]]|title=Listado de Comunidades|url=http://fichacomunidad.ine.gob.bo/ |date=2014}}<!-- auto-translated by Module:CS1 translator --></ref>

== History ==
The town was established by [[Okinawan diaspora|Okinawan immigrants]] after the end of the [[Second World War]], and during its peak in the mid-1960s consisted of 565 families and over 3,000 Okinawans in total.{{Sfnp|Miyahira|Petrucci|2015|p=557}}<ref>{{Cite web |last=Mar 2014 |first=Akemi Kikumura Yano / 14 |title=Bolivia - Migration Historical Overview |url=http://www.discovernikkei.org/en/journal/2014/3/14/bolivia/ |access-date=2023-01-12 |website=Discover Nikkei |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Okinawa, Bolivia |url=https://theworld.org/stories/2009-07-06/okinawa-bolivia |access-date=2023-01-12 |website=The World from PRX |language=en}}</ref>
