'''Jesús de Machaca Municipality''' is the sixth [[Municipalities of Bolivia|municipal section]] of the [[Ingavi Province]] in the  [[La Paz Department (Bolivia)|La Paz Department]] in [[Bolivia]]. It was created by Law No. 2351 on May 7, 2002, during the presidency of [[Jorge Quiroga Ramírez]].<ref>[http://www.legislacionmunicipal.fam.bo/Archivo/Docs/Leyes/Ley_2351.pdf www.legislacionmunicipal.fam.bo] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120326031801/http://www.legislacionmunicipal.fam.bo/Archivo/Docs/Leyes/Ley_2351.pdf |date=2012-03-26 }} (Spanish)</ref>  Its seat is [[Jesús de Machaca]].

== Geography ==
Jesús de Machaca lies south of [[Lake Titicaca]]. The [[Chilla-Kimsa Chata mountain range|Chilla-Kimsa Chata]] mountain range traverses the municipality. Some of the highest mountains of the municipality are listed below:<ref>IGM map 1:50,000 5844-III Guaqui</ref><ref>IGM map 1:50,000 5843-I Sacacani</ref><ref>IGM map 1:50,000 Tiahuanacu 5844-II</ref>

== Division ==
The municipality consists of the following ten [[Cantons of Bolivia|cantons]]:<ref>[http://www.ine.gob.bo National Institute of Statistics of Bolivia] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110924073930/http://www.ine.gob.bo/ |date=2011-09-24 }}</ref>
* Aguallamaya - 1,694 inhabitants (''2001'')
* Chama - 1,278 inhabitants
* Cuipa España de Machaca - 662 inhabitants
* Jesús de Machaca - 862 inhabitants
* Kalla Tupac Katari  - 2,669 inhabitants
* Khonkho San Salvador - 755 inhabitants
* Mejillones de Machaca - 845 inhabitants
* Santa Ana de Machaca - 363 inhabitants
* Santo Domingo de Machaca - 588 inhabitants
* Villa Asunción de Machaca - 3,244 inhabitants

== The people ==
The people are predominantly [[Indigenous peoples of the Americas|indigenous]] citizens of [[Aymara people|Aymara]] descent.<ref>obd.descentralizacion.gov.bo/municipal/fichas/ (inactive)</ref>

== Places of interest ==
Some of the tourist attractions of the municipality are:<ref>[http://www.gobernacionlapaz.gob.bo:8001/simit/sistema/ficha_turistica/iframelapaz.php www.gobernacionlapaz.gob.bo] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110509054006/http://www.gobernacionlapaz.gob.bo:8001/simit/sistema/ficha_turistica/iframelapaz.php |date=2011-05-09 }} Tourist guide by the government of the department</ref>

* The archaeological site of [[Qhunqhu Wankani]] in Jesús de Machaca Canton
* Jesús de Machaca, an [[Indigenous peoples of the Americas|indigenous]] community
* Yakayuni saltflats in Jesús de Machaca Canton where salt exploitation is possible
* Uru Iruito community in Jesús de Machaca Canton and the Urus Iruito museum in Jesús de Machaca
