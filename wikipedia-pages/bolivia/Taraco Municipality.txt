'''Taraco Municipality''' is the seventh [[Municipalities of Bolivia|municipal section]] of the [[Ingavi Province]] in the  [[La Paz Department (Bolivia)|La Paz Department]], [[Bolivia]]. Most of its area is situated on [[Taraco|Taraco Peninsula]] jutting into [[Lake Wiñaymarka]], the southern branch of [[Lake Titicaca]]. Its seat is [[Taraco, La Paz|Taraco]].
[[File:Lago Menor o Huiñamarca Perú Bolivia Satelital map 68.85829W 16.png|thumb|left|180px|Taraco Peninsula (in the middle) in Lake Wiñaymarka (Titicaca)]]

== Division ==
The municipality is divided into the following [[Cantons of Bolivia|cantons]]:<ref>[http://www.ine.gob.bo National Institute of Statistics of Bolivia]</ref>
* Santa Rosa de Taraco - 1,071 inhabitants ''(2001)'' 
* Taraco - 4,851 inhabitants

== The people ==
The people are predominantly [[Indigenous peoples of the Americas|indigenous]] citizens of [[Aymara people|Aymara]] descent.<ref>obd.descentralizacion.gov.bo/municipal/fichas/ (inactive)</ref>

== Places of interest ==
Some of the tourist attractions of the municipalities are:<ref>[http://www.gobernacionlapaz.gob.bo:8001/simit/sistema/ficha_turistica/iframelapaz.php www.gobernacionlapaz.gob.bo] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110509054006/http://www.gobernacionlapaz.gob.bo:8001/simit/sistema/ficha_turistica/iframelapaz.php |date=2011-05-09 }} Tourist guide by the government of the department</ref>
* the archaeological sites on Taraco Peninsula:
** [[Chiripa (archeological site)|Chiripa]] and its museum
** Kala Uyuni, Pumani and Achacachi Coacollo in Coacollo
** Iwawi (Kolata Quenacache, Ojje Puku, Awichu and Chojna Qala) in Higuagui Grande
* Sikuya Island in Taraco Canton
* The church of Taraco dating from 1767 
* Taraco museum in Taraco Canton
