[[File:Vista de la plaza de turco.JPG|thumb|right|200px|Main square of Turco]]
'''Turco Municipality''' is the second [[Municipalities of Bolivia|municipal section]] of the [[Sajama Province]] in the [[Oruro Department]] in [[Bolivia]], and was founded on February 15, 1957.<ref name="Plan Turco">[http://www.apemin.eu/PDM/PDMTURCO.pdf Plan de desarollo municipal Municipio de Turco] (Spanish)</ref> Its seat is Turco, situated 154&nbsp;km west of [[Oruro, Bolivia|Oruro]] at an altitude of 3,860 m. The municipality covers an area of 3,973&nbsp;km², not taking into account the area of Laca Laca Canton.<ref name = "Plan Turco" /><ref>The data of the area of Laca Laca Canton were erroneously added to the Choquecota Municipality during the census of 2001.</ref>

It is bordered to the north by the Curahuara de Carangas Municipality and [[San Pedro de Totora Province]], to the south by the [[Litoral Province (Bolivia)|Litoral]] and [[Sabaya Province|Sabaya]] Provinces, to the west by [[Chile]] and to the east by the [[Carangas Province]] (Qhurqhi (Corque) and Chuqi Quta (Choquecota) Municipalities).

== Geography ==
The most important rivers are Sajama, Cosapa, Lauca, K'uwiri and Qullpa Jawira (also named ''río Turco'', Turco River) .

A couple of volcanoes and mountains of the [[Cordillera Occidental (Bolivia)|Bolivian Western Mountain Range]] like [[Uqi Uqini]] and the volcanic complex of [[Kimsa Chata (Bolivia-Chile)|Kimsa Chata]], a group of three mountains named [[Umurata]], [[Acotango]] and Capurata in the west of the municipality are making up the natural border of Turco Municipality with Chile. Other mountains are listed below:<ref>Bolivian IGM map 1:50,000 Chachacomani Hoja 5838-IV, Cerro Uyarani Hoja 5838-I,  Estancia Antin Curahuara 5939-III, Estancia Agua Rica Hoja 5839-II, Estancia Laguna 5839-III, Estancia Marcarani 5939-IV, Estancia Miraflores 5838-II, Nevado Quimsachata Hoja 5738-I</ref>

==Flora and fauna==
[[File:Polylepis Tarapacana 1.jpg|thumb|right|200px|''Polylepis tarapacana'' near [[Nevado Sajama|Sajama]] volcano]]
[[File:Tunilla soehrensii 1.jpg|thumb|right|200px|''Tunilla soehrensii'']]
Part of Cosapa Canton is situated within [[Sajama National Park]].<ref>[http://www.condesan.org/unesco/Cap%2016%20MIREYA%20SAJAMAColca.pdf Mireya Muñoz, El Parque Nacional Sajama: Un caso de paisaje cultural en el altiplano de Bolivia] p. 194</ref> This park was created to conserve the rare woods of the queñua species ''[[Polylepis tarapacana]]'' and to protect species of wild flora and fauna like [[yareta]] ''(Azorella compacta)'', [[Andean hairy armadillo]] ''(Chaetophractus nationi)'', [[Darwin's rhea]] ''(Pterocnemia pennata)'', [[vicuña]] ''(Vicugna vicugna)'', [[taruca]] ''(Hippocamelus antisensis)'', [[Andean mountain cat]] ''(Felis jacobita)'' and  [[cougar]] or puma ''(Felis concolor)'', all of them occurring in the municipality,<ref name = "Plan Turco" /> as well as their [[habitat]].

== The people ==
Most of the people in the municipality are of [[Aymara people|Aymara]] descent known as Carangas. They are mostly bilingual. According to census 2001 the main languages spoken are [[Spanish language|Spanish]] (3,544 speakers) and [[Aymara language|Aymara]] (3,259 speakers).<ref name = "INE" />

== Transport ==
[[File:Ruta 4 (Bolivia) con el Nevado Sajama de fondo.jpg|thumb|right|200px|Route 4 ''(Ruta 4)'' near Tambo Quemado with Sajama volcano in the background]]
The international transit road from Oruro to Chile crosses Turco Municipality leading to [[Tambo Quemado]] situated in the Chachacomani Canton in the north-western part of the municipality <ref name = "Plan Turco" /><ref>In the map below Tambo Quemado erroneously is shown as part of Lagunas Canton in the Curahuara de Carangas Municipality (Sajama Province), but actually belongs to Turco Municipality.</ref> at the border between Chile and Bolivia.
