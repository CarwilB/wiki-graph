'''Tarabuco  Municipality''' is the first [[Municipalities of Bolivia|municipal section]] of the [[Yamparáez Province]] in the [[Chuquisaca Department]], [[Bolivia]]. Its seat is [[Tarabuco]]. Its population is overwhelmingly indigenous: of 10,530 people 15 or older in the 2001 Census, 9,834 (or 93,40%) self-identified with an indigenous people; 9,779 to the Quechua people, 29 [[Aymara people|Aymara]], 15 to another indigenous people, and 7 as [[Guarani people|Guaraní]].<ref name=TarabucoMA>"[http://www.autonomia.gob.bo/portal3/content/municipio-de-tarabuco Municipio de Tarabuco] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110706150401/http://www.autonomia.gob.bo/portal3/content/municipio-de-tarabuco |date=2011-07-06 }}," Ministerio de Autonomías.</ref> On 6 December 2009, its residents voted in favor of indigenous autonomy for the municipality, with a vote of 90.8%.<ref name=Avanzan>"Las autonomías indígenas avanzan a paso lento per seguro, entre consensos e interrogantes" ''Diálogos en Democracia'', 21 March 2010 (Supplement to ''Pulso Bolivia'').</ref>
