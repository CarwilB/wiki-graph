'''Chimoré Municipality''' is the fourth [[Municipalities of Bolivia|municipal section]] of the [[Carrasco Province (Bolivia)|Carrasco Province]] in the [[Cochabamba Department]], [[Bolivia]]. Its seat is [[Chimoré, Cochabamba|Chimoré]].

This municipality was created in 1984 by Law number 633 of September 13, 1984, encompassing the towns of San Isidro, [[Senda III]], La Victoria, Santa Rosa, Todos Santos, Puerto Alegre, Entre Rios Tacuaral, [[Puerto Aurora]], [[Senda F]], Senda D, Senda B y [[Cesarzama]].

It is bordered by the rivers [[Ichilo River|Ichilo]], [[Chimoré River|Chimoré]] and [[Chapare River|Chapare]].

== Subdivision ==
The municipality consists of only one [[:Category:Cantons of Bolivia|canton]], Chimoré Canton. It is identical to the municipality.

== The people ==
The people are predominantly [[Indigenous peoples of the Americas|indigenous]] citizens of [[Quechuas|Quechuan]] descent.<ref>obd.descentralizacion.gov.bo</ref> There are also groups of [[Yuracaré people|Yuracaré]] along Chapare River like in the communities of Ibare, Puerto Cochabamba and Nueva Esperanza.<ref>[http://www.amazonia.bo/mas_detalle_proi.php?id_contenido=29 www.amazonia.bo] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110209054907/http://www.amazonia.bo/mas_detalle_proi.php?id_contenido=29 |date=2011-02-09 }}</ref>
