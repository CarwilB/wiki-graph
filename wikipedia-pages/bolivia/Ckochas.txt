'''Ckochas''' is a town and [[Municipalities of Bolivia|municipality]] in [[Bolivia]], located in the Linares Province in the eastern part of the [[Potosí Department]]. The municipality has an area of 2,040.2 km<sup>2</sup> and a population of 15,812 inhabitants (according to the [[2012 Bolivian census|2012 INE Census]]).<ref name=ine>{{cite web|title=Censo INE|url=http://censosbolivia.ine.gob.bo/censofichacomunidad/c_listadof/listar_comunidades|publisher=Instituto Nacional de Estadística|language=Spanish|access-date=May 23, 2019|archive-date=November 7, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141107025542/http://censosbolivia.ine.gob.bo/censofichacomunidad/c_listadof/listar_comunidades|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Ckochas is one of the youngest municipalities in Bolivia, having separated from the municipality of Puna. It was created by Decree Law 3462 on August 15, 2006.<ref name=creación>{{cite news|title=Se creó un nuevo municipio en la provincia Linares en Potosí|url=https://www.noticiasfides.com/nacional/sociedad/se-creo-un-nuevo-municipio-en-la-provincia-linares-en-potosi-192096|access-date=July 16, 2019|work=Agencia de Noticias Fides|date=September 29, 2006}}</ref>

Within its territory, on the border with the [[Chuquisaca Department]], lies the Fortress of Oronckota (Fortaleza de Oronckota), which is one of the most important archaeological sites in the Potosí Department.<ref name=oronckota>{{cite news|last1=Fuertes Callapino|first1=Ivert Elvis|title=Oronckota, el valle arqueológico y edén del maní|url=https://www.paginasiete.bo/gente/2020/1/29/oronckota-el-valle-arqueologico-eden-del-mani-244881.html|access-date=February 9, 2020|work=Página Siete|date=January 29, 2020|language=Spanish|archive-date=January 31, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200131233125/https://www.paginasiete.bo/gente/2020/1/29/oronckota-el-valle-arqueologico-eden-del-mani-244881.html|url-status=dead}}</ref>

== Toponymy ==
Ckochas is a word from the [[Southern Quechua|Quechua]] language meaning "high lands near the mountain range".<ref name=ckochas>{{cite news|title=Ckochas celebra 8 años de creación con la inauguración de un coliseo e infraestructura educativa|url=https://comunicacion.gob.bo/?q=20140815/16386|access-date=July 16, 2019|work=Ministerio de Comunicación|date=August 15, 2012|language=Spanish}}</ref>

== Geography ==
The municipality has an irregular and diverse topography and relief, featuring mountains, mountain ranges, high hills, plains, and deep valleys. The mountain ranges extend from south to north, and the Turuchipa and Mataca valleys are classified as alluvial plains. According to the classification of ecological zones, Ckochas is composed of four: high Andean, Puna, Prepuna, and valleys. It has an annual rainfall of between 300 mm and 500 mm.

It is bordered to the north by the municipality of Betanzos in the [[Cornelio Saavedra Province]], to the east and south by the municipalities of Icla, [[Tarvita]], and [[San Lucas, Bolivia|San Lucas]] in the Chuquisaca Department, and to the west by the municipality of Puna.

== Demographics ==
According to the [[2024 Bolivian census]], the population of the municipality of Ckochas is 14,338 inhabitants.<ref name="resultados2024">{{cite web|url=https://nubecpv.ine.gob.bo/index.php/apps/onlyoffice/s/fBDwRbxQKgAxegF?fileId=548802|title=Resultados CPV 2024|access-date=April 29, 2025|date=September 2024|publisher=Instituto Nacional de Estadística de Bolivia}}</ref>
