'''Santiago de Huata''' is a town and municipality in [[Omasuyos Province]], [[La Paz Department (Bolivia)|La Paz Department]], [[Bolivia]]. It is located in the eastern shore of [[Lake Titicaca]], at an average elevation of 3911 meters above the sea level.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Santiago de Huata topographic map, elevation, terrain |url=https://en-gb.topographic-map.com/map-fd3z4s/Santiago-de-Huata/?center=-16.05695,-68.81651 |access-date=19 October 2023 |website=Topographic maps |language=en}}</ref> The municipality has a population of 9,158 in 2020.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Santiago de Huata (Municipality, Bolivia) - Population Statistics, Charts, Map and Location |url=https://www.citypopulation.de/en/bolivia/admin/la_paz/020205__santiago_de_huata/ |access-date=19 October 2023 |website=www.citypopulation.de}}</ref>

== Climate ==
Santiago de Huata has a [[Tundra climate|Tundra Climate]] (ET) under the [[Köppen climate classification|Köppen Climate Classification]]. It receives the most rainfall in January, with an average precipitation of 222 mm; and the least rainfall in July, with an average precipitation of 17 mm.
