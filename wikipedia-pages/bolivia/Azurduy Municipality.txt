'''Azurduy Municipality''' ('''Municipio de Azurduy''',<ref name="Jefatura" /> '''Villa Azurduy Municipality''') is the first [[Municipalities of Bolivia|municipal section]] of [[Azurduy Province]] in the [[Chuquisaca Department]] of [[Bolivia]]. Its seat is [[Azurduy, Bolivia|Villa Azurduy]].  In 2012 the population was 10,594 persons.  The municipality is very poor; in 2010 93% were below the poverty line, and 75% were in [[extreme poverty]].<ref name="INE-Muni-2001">{{Cite web|title=Atlas Municipal: Azurduy|publisher=Instituto Nacional de Estadística de Bolivia|url=http://www.ine.gob.bo/pdf/AtlasMunicipal/010201.pdf|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140205193315/http://www.ine.gob.bo/pdf/AtlasMunicipal/010201.pdf|archivedate=5 February 2014|url-status=live}}</ref>  In 2001 there were seven automobiles (including trucks) in the municipality.<ref name="INE-Muni-2001" />

== Geography ==
The municipality is in the mountains of the [[Cordillera Oriental (Bolivia)|Cordillera Oriental]] of the [[Andes]], and is bordered on the west by the [[Pilcomayo River]]. The mountain ranges and the rivers are generally oriented north-south. Due to its elevation, the municipality experiences a wide range of temperatures between day and night. The average annual temperature is {{Convert|14|C}}, the average monthly values varying between {{Convert|10|C}} in June and July to {{Convert|16|C}} in December and January. Annual precipitation barely reaches {{convert|550|mm|in|1|abbr=on}}, with less than {{convert|10|mm|in|1|abbr=on}} monthly in the dry season of May to August, and with the highest rainfall, {{convert|100|to|110|mm|in|1|abbr=on}} per month, between December and February.

To the north Azurduy Municipality is bordered by Tarvita Municipality, also of Azurduy Province, and El Villar Municipality of [[Tomina Province]]; to the east by [[Hernando Siles Province]], and to the west and south by [[Nor Cinti Province]]. There are 132 settlements in the municipality<ref name="INE-Muni-2001" /> with thirty-six villages, of which only eight are accessible by road.<ref name="ACLO-2012">{{Cite web|date=July 2012 |title=Consejo Municipal de Azurduy Elabora Cronograma para la Construcción de la Carta Orgánica Municipal (COM) |language=Spanish |publisher=Acción Cultural Loyola (ACLO) |url=http://aclo.org.bo/bolivia/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=608:consejo-municipal-de-azurduy-elabora-cronograma-para-la-construccion-de-la-carta-organica-municipal-com&catid=51:noticias-chuquisaca&Itemid=496 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140205220901/http://aclo.org.bo/bolivia/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=608%3Aconsejo-municipal-de-azurduy-elabora-cronograma-para-la-construccion-de-la-carta-organica-municipal-com&catid=51%3Anoticias-chuquisaca&Itemid=496 |archivedate=5 February 2014 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Economics==
The people are primarily employed in agriculture and livestock raising, although there is some mining.<ref name="INE-Muni-2001" />  Major crops include corn, wheat, potatoes, barley and peppers. Sheep, cattle, goats, pigs, horses and poultry are raised.<ref name="ACLO-2012" />

==The people==
The people are predominantly [[Indigenous peoples of the Americas|indigenous]] citizens of [[Quechuas|Quechuan]] descent.<ref name="INE-Muni-2001" />

===Population===
The population of the municipality of Azurduy has not increased from 1992 to 2012.<ref name="Población">{{Cite web|title=Chuquisaca: Proyecciones de Población por Provincias y Municipios, según Sexo, Edades Simples y Años Calendario, Período 2000 - 2010 |date=August 2005 |language=Spanish |location=La Paz, Bolivia |publisher=Instituto Nacional de Estadística de Bolivia |url=http://www.ine.gob.bo/PDF/DIFD/ProyeccionesPoblacionProvinciasMunicipiosChuquisaca.pdf |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304055634/http://www.ine.gob.bo/PDF/DIFD/ProyeccionesPoblacionProvinciasMunicipiosChuquisaca.pdf |archivedate=2016-03-04 }}</ref>
* 1992: 10,818 inhabitants (census)
* 2001: 11,349 inhabitants (census)
* 2012: 10,594 inhabitants (census)
At the 2001 census, the literacy rate was 61.4 percent, the life expectancy of the newborn was 57.0 years, and the infant mortality rate was 9.3 per cent. 60.9 percent of the population spoke Spanish, 55.8 percent spoke Quechua (2001). 90.3 percent of the population had access to electricity, while 89.7 percent lived without sanitation facilities.<ref name="INE-Muni-2001" /><ref name="Población" />

==Cantons==
The municipality is divided into three [[Cantons of Bolivia|cantons]].<ref name="INE-Muni-2001" />
* Las Casas Canton, with administrative seat in [[Torrecillas, Bolivia|Torrecillas]]
* Azurduy Canton, with administrative seat in [[Azurduy, Bolivia|Villa Azurduy]]
* Antonio Lopez Canton, with administrative seat in [[San Antonio, Azurduy|San Antonio]]
