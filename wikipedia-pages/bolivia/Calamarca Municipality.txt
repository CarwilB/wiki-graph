'''Calamarca''' (in Hispanicized spelling) or '''Qala Marka'''<ref>{{Cite book |author=Morales, Salustiano Ayma |year=2011 |title=Aymara aru thakhinchawi |language=ay |location=La Paz, Bolivia |publisher=Arcus Industria Gráfica for the Ministry of Education |page=25 |url=http://upiip.minedu.gob.bo/paginas/modulos_sistema/gestor_biblioteca/material/73/Aymara-Euphemia.pdf |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160226164804/http://upiip.minedu.gob.bo/paginas/modulos_sistema/gestor_biblioteca/material/73/Aymara-Euphemia.pdf# |archive-date=2016-02-26 |url-status=dead |access-date=2016-02-26 }}</ref> ([[Aymara language|Aymara]] ''qala'' stone, ''marka'' village, town, place, land, "place of stone") is the fourth [[Municipalities of Bolivia|municipal section]] of [[Aroma Province]] in the [[La Paz Department (Bolivia)|La Paz Department]] of [[Bolivia]]. It is located on the [[Altiplano]] and its [[Administrative centre|seat]] is the town of [[Calamarca]].<ref name="gov">{{Cite web|title=Gobierno Autónomo Municipal de Calamarca|language=es |url=http://kalamarca.blogspot.com/2013/09/donde-esta-calamarca-de-bolivia.html |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20160226180008/http://kalamarca.blogspot.com/2013/09/donde-esta-calamarca-de-bolivia.html |archive-date=26 February 2016 |url-status=live}}</ref>

The municipality was created by law on 21 December 1948. At present it is divided into seven [[Cantons of Bolivia|cantons]] (''cantones''): Calamarca, Vilaque Copata, Sivicani, Ajoya, Villa Carmen de Caluyo, [[San Antonio de Senkata]] and Cosmini.<ref name="gov" />
