Bolivia CEL comparison — 2001 vs 2024

Background

This document compares the Condición Étnico-Lingüística (CEL) classification of Bolivian municipalities between the 2001 census (as published by Albó & Romero 2009) and the 2024 census (CPV 2024). The CEL scale assigns each person a level 0–7 based on indigenous self-identification, language use, childhood language, and Castellano use. Municipalities are further classified into letter groups A–I based on threshold shares at CEL ≥ 5, ≥ 4, and ≥ 2.

Group CEL ≥ 5 CEL ≥ 4 CEL ≥ 2
A ≥ 90% ≥ 90% ≥ 90%
B > 67% > 80% > 90%
C > 50% > 67% > 67%
D > 33% > 50% > 67%
E — — > 75%
F — — > 50%
G — — > 33%
H — — > 10%
I < 10% in all categories

Assign letter groups to 2001 data

The 2001 data has CEL percentages in columns cel_0_nnn through cel_7_sss and pre-computed threshold columns crit_orig_5a7, crit_orig_4a7, crit_orig_2a7. We use these to assign letter groups.

assign_albo_group <- function(cel_5plus, cel_4plus, cel_2plus) {
  case_when(
    cel_5plus >= 90 & cel_4plus >= 90 & cel_2plus >= 90 ~ "A",
    cel_5plus >  67 & cel_4plus >  80 & cel_2plus >  90 ~ "B",
    cel_5plus >  50 & cel_4plus >  67 & cel_2plus >  67 ~ "C",
    cel_5plus >  33 & cel_4plus >  50 & cel_2plus >  67 ~ "D",
    cel_2plus >  75                                      ~ "E",
    cel_2plus >  50                                      ~ "F",
    cel_2plus >  33                                      ~ "G",
    cel_2plus >  10                                      ~ "H",
    TRUE                                                 ~ "I"
  )
}

Build comparison dataset

Join 2001 and 2024 on municipal code. Only the 327 municipalities present in both censuses are included; 16 post-2001 municipalities appear only in 2024.

comparison <- albo_2001 |>
  select(codigo, municipality_2001 = municipality, department,
         group_2001, cel5_2001 = crit_orig_5a7, cel4_2001 = crit_orig_4a7,
         cel2_2001 = crit_orig_2a7) |>
  inner_join(
    cel_2024 |>
      select(cod_mun, group_2024, cel5_2024 = cel_5plus, cel4_2024 = cel_4plus,
             cel2_2024 = cel_2plus, n_2024 = n_total),
    by = c("codigo" = "cod_mun")
  ) |>
  left_join(pop |> select(cod.mun, mun_name, t_2001, t_2012),
            by = c("codigo" = "cod.mun"))

comparison <- comparison |>
  mutate(
    group_2001 = factor(group_2001, levels = LETTERS[1:9]),
    group_2024 = factor(group_2024, levels = LETTERS[1:9]),
    # Numeric rank for direction of change
    rank_2001 = as.integer(group_2001),
    rank_2024 = as.integer(group_2024),
    direction = case_when(
      rank_2024 < rank_2001 ~ "More indigenous",
      rank_2024 > rank_2001 ~ "Less indigenous",
      TRUE                  ~ "No change"
    )
  )

Urban indigenous populations in capital cities

Bolivia’s 10 departmental capitals plus El Alto experienced dramatic declines in indigenous populations across all three CEL thresholds between 2001 and 2024. Unlike most municipalities which shifted modestly, these major urban centers saw wholesale language loss and identity change, reducing their indigenous populations by millions and cutting indigenous shares roughly in half.

# Visualize city-level CEL percentages over time
urban_cities |>
  select(mun_name, cel2_2001, cel2_2024, cel4_2001, cel4_2024, cel5_2001, cel5_2024) |>
  pivot_longer(starts_with("cel"), 
               names_to = "threshold_year", 
               values_to = "pct") |>
  separate(threshold_year, into = c("cel", "year"), sep = "_") |>
  mutate(
    threshold = factor(paste0("CEL ≥ ", cel), levels = c("CEL ≥ 2", "CEL ≥ 4", "CEL ≥ 5")),
    year = if_else(year == "2001", "2001", "2024"),
    mun_name = fct_reorder(mun_name, pct, .fun = mean, .desc = TRUE)
  ) |>
  ggplot(aes(x = mun_name, y = pct, fill = year)) +
  geom_col(position = "dodge", width = 0.7) +
  facet_wrap(~ threshold, nrow = 1) +
  scale_fill_manual(values = c("2001" = "#2166ac", "2024" = "#f46d43"), name = NULL) +
  scale_y_continuous(labels = scales::percent_format(scale = 1)) +
  labs(
    title = "Indigenous populations in capital cities: 2001 vs 2024",
    subtitle = "Percentage of population at each CEL threshold",
    x = NULL, y = "% of city population"
  ) +
  theme_minimal(base_size = 11) +
  theme(
    axis.text.x = element_text(angle = 45, hjust = 1),
    legend.position = "bottom",
    panel.spacing = unit(0.5, "lines")
  )

Key findings

  • CEL ≥ 2 (any indigenous dimension): 2.29M → 1.32M indigenous; share dropped from 59% to 26%
  • CEL ≥ 4 (indigenous identity): 2.19M → 1.18M indigenous; share dropped from 56% to 23%
  • CEL ≥ 5 (identity + language): 1.05M → 0.70M indigenous; share dropped from 27% to 14%

All major urban centers experienced dramatic decline in indigenous populations across every threshold. Cities like Santa Cruz and Tarija saw particularly severe shifts, with indigenous shares falling below 10% at the CEL ≥ 2 threshold. The urban-rural indigenous divide has become one of Bolivia’s most pronounced demographic features.
